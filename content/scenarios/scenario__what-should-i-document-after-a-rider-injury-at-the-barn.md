# What should i document after a rider injury at the barn

## Quick answer
Short answer: Before you demand payment, refuse payment, threaten suit, or send a hard response, gather the contract, the money trail, the timeline, and the best objective proof of what was said and what happened.

## What this means
This page is for horse owners, trainers, barns, and equine businesses trying to avoid making the dispute worse while facts are still being assembled. The goal is to help you organize the file before you escalate.

## What people often miss
People often move too fast. They send a heated message, make a refund demand, or threaten a legal position before they have lined up the signed documents, payment records, text history, and state-specific rules that actually control leverage.

## How this usually plays out
These situations usually turn on four things: the controlling document, the cleanest timeline, the best proof of performance or nonperformance, and any clause that shifts payment, risk, notice, authority, or refund rights.

## Where this can go wrong
The common failure is acting from memory instead of from the record. Another failure is assuming one screenshot or one invoice settles the issue when the real problem is spread across the contract, messages, vet or care records, and timing.

## General next step framing
The next move is usually to build one clean dispute packet before you negotiate, answer, or escalate: signed agreement, payment record, timeline, key communications, and a short list of unanswered factual questions.
